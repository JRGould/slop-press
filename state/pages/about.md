SlopPress is a tiny experiment in radical compression: one markdown file, one server, one improvised brain.

It takes the usual sprawl of a CMS — routers, templates, plugins, databases, queues, dashboards, and the lingering fear of a bad deploy — and folds it into a single functional unit that just makes the page as you ask it for it.

The result is intentionally modest and a little uncanny. The copy is fresh each time, the layout stays calm, and the source of truth remains beautifully small.
